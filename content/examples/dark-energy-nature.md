## Falsification Prompt

**If dark energy is a static cosmological constant (w = −1), we would expect to observe:**
- Equation-of-state parameter w consistent with −1 across all redshifts
- DESI result not confirmed by independent instruments
- No variation in dark energy density over cosmic time

**If dark energy is a dynamic field (w ≠ −1, evolving over time), we would expect to observe:**
- Consistent measurement of w deviating from −1 across independent surveys
- Evolution of w with redshift measurable by Euclid, Vera Rubin Observatory, or Roman Space Telescope
- Theoretical models of dynamic dark energy making additional testable predictions

**If dark energy reflects modified gravity rather than a new energy component, we would expect to observe:**
- Deviations from General Relativity at cosmological scales detectable in gravitational lensing surveys
- Inconsistencies between different probes of the expansion history

**Note:** The DESI result is currently the most significant challenge to the cosmological constant interpretation. Its confirmation or refutation by independent instruments within the next five to ten years will be a decisive epistemic moment for this question.
